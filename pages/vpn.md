# VPN

VPN Security Standards and Verified Providers Guide

This guide reflects current security expectations for consumer VPN services. All claims should be verified directly through the official audit reports linked on each provider's website before subscribing.

## What Defines a Secure VPN?
A secure VPN service should meet the following criteria:

* Independently audited no logs policy
* Transparent architecture and preferably open source applications
* RAM only server infrastructure
* Privacy focused jurisdiction
* Modern protocols such as WireGuard with post quantum enhancements where available

## Critical VPN Selection Considerations

### Free VPN Services
Most free VPN services monetize user data or lack independent audits.  
**Exception:** Proton VPN Free plan is covered by the same audited privacy standards as paid tiers.

### Post Quantum Cryptography
Some providers have begun integrating post quantum resistant key exchange mechanisms to mitigate harvest now decrypt later risks.  
NordVPN has implemented post quantum enhancements within supported applications.  
Other providers are researching or testing similar integrations.

### Jurisdiction Overview
Privacy favorable jurisdictions include: Switzerland, Panama, British Virgin Islands, Sweden, Gibraltar

Countries within intelligence sharing alliances such as Five Eyes, Nine Eyes, and Fourteen Eyes may present higher legal exposure risks depending on enforcement conditions.
