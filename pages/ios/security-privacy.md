# iOS Security & Privacy

iOS security is strongest when the device is updated, locked with a strong passcode, backed up, and not modified by untrusted profiles, certificates, or jailbreak tweaks.

## Baseline Rules

- Keep iOS updated.
- Use a strong passcode.
- Keep Find My enabled.
- Turn on Stolen Device Protection.
- Use two-factor authentication.
- Use passkeys where supported.
- Review app permissions monthly.
- Avoid jailbreaking your main phone.
- Avoid unknown configuration profiles and enterprise certificates.
- Back up before updates, repairs, jailbreak work, or device trade-in.

## Update Priority

Apple says keeping software up to date is the single most important thing you can do for Apple product security. Apple has also warned users to update iOS to protect against web attacks targeting out-of-date iOS versions.

If a phone cannot receive current updates, reduce its role:

- Do not use it for banking.
- Do not use it as your only 2FA device.
- Do not keep sensitive work accounts on it.
- Prefer offline, lab, media, or spare-device use.

## Stolen Device Protection

Stolen Device Protection adds extra protection if someone steals both your iPhone and passcode.

Use it because it can require biometric authentication and, for some actions, a security delay before account or device changes.

Set it to always require additional security if you want stronger protection even at familiar locations.

## Advanced Data Protection

Advanced Data Protection increases end-to-end encryption coverage for many iCloud data categories, including device backups, Photos, Notes, iCloud Drive, and more.

Before enabling:

- Set a recovery contact or recovery key.
- Store recovery information somewhere safe.
- Update all devices on the Apple Account.
- Understand that Apple cannot recover protected data if you lose account access and recovery methods.

## Lockdown Mode

Lockdown Mode is for people at risk of highly targeted attacks, such as journalists, activists, political figures, executives, and high-risk researchers.

It limits some websites, attachments, FaceTime behavior, Apple service invitations, device connections, wireless behavior, and configuration profile or MDM enrollment. Most users do not need it, but it is useful for high-risk situations.

## Permission Review

Review:

- Location.
- Photos.
- Contacts.
- Camera.
- Microphone.
- Bluetooth.
- Local Network.
- Motion and Fitness.
- Files and folders.
- Tracking.
- Notifications.

Prefer limited Photos access and approximate location when enough.

## Avoid

- Unknown VPNs.
- Keyboard apps from unknown developers.
- Profiles from "free app" sites.
- Jailbreak detection bypass tools on a sensitive phone.
- Piracy repos.
- Sharing Apple Account passwords or device passcodes.
- Leaving old trusted computers paired forever.
