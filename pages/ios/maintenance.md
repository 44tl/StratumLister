# iOS Maintenance

iOS maintenance should be boring: update, back up, review permissions, remove unused apps, and keep account recovery healthy.

## Monthly Routine

- Check for iOS or iPadOS updates.
- Update apps.
- Confirm iCloud Backup or encrypted computer backup is current.
- Review Apple Account trusted devices.
- Review Find My devices.
- Review app permissions.
- Remove unused apps and profiles.
- Check battery health.
- Check available storage.
- Restart if the device has been acting strange.

## Update Routine

Before major updates:

1. Confirm backup.
2. Check free storage.
3. Charge or connect power.
4. Read release notes if the device is critical.
5. Update.
6. Reboot if needed.
7. Update apps.
8. Check battery and app behavior for a day or two.

For jailbreak candidates, do not update until you decide whether preserving the current firmware matters more than security patches.

## Storage Cleanup

Good cleanup:

- Delete downloaded videos, podcasts, and music.
- Clear large Messages attachments.
- Offload unused apps.
- Export old photos and videos.
- Delete duplicate downloads in Files.

Avoid:

- Cleaner apps.
- Profile-based "optimizer" tools.
- Deleting app data before confirming the app syncs or backs up.

## Battery Maintenance

- Keep the phone out of heat.
- Use optimized charging features when available.
- Avoid cheap or damaged chargers and cables.
- Replace the battery when health is the issue.
- Check battery usage by app before blaming iOS.

## Account Maintenance

- Keep trusted phone numbers current.
- Keep a recovery contact or recovery key current.
- Remove old trusted devices.
- Review Family Sharing members.
- Review subscriptions.
- Review Sign in with Apple apps.
- Keep password manager and 2FA recovery documented.

## Jailbroken Device Routine

- Do not update impulsively.
- Keep the jailbreak app/tool version noted.
- Update tweaks one at a time.
- Avoid piracy repos.
- Keep a clean backup from before the jailbreak.
- Remove or disable tweaks before troubleshooting normal app issues.
- Expect detection by banking, streaming, and work apps.

## Replace Or Retire

Retire a device from sensitive use when:

- It no longer receives current security updates.
- The battery is swollen or unreliable.
- Face ID or Touch ID fails and repair is not practical.
- The charging port is unreliable.
- It cannot run required banking, 2FA, or work apps safely.

Old iPhones and iPads can still be useful for offline media, HomeKit dashboards, test devices, music, cameras, or jailbreak labs.
