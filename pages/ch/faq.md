# FAQ

Common questions regarding Stratum's philosophy, methodology, and safety standards.

## Is Stratum a debloat tool?
No. Stratum is a technical knowledge base. We do not provide "one-click" batch scripts or black-box optimization utilities. Our goal is to provide the knowledge required to make **reversible, explainable, and manual** system changes.

## Are all links on Stratum verified?
No. While we curate our links carefully, only those with a **verification badge** have undergone a full manual review by the maintainers. Always exercise caution when downloading software from external sources; verification is a snapshot in time and does not account for future updates to third-party sites.

## What is the first thing I should do before optimizing?
**Create a full System Restore point.** Optimization involves changing system variables; having a fallback mechanism is the most critical step in any technical workflow. Ensure no Windows Updates are pending before you begin.

## Why does Stratum speak against "Gaming ISOs"?
Many custom "stripped" Windows ISOs remove critical security components, break Windows Update, and introduce unverified third-party binaries. Stratum focuses on optimizing official Windows installations to maintain a high level of security and stability.

## Should I disable Windows Defender for better FPS?
**No.** For the vast majority of users, the performance gain from disabling baseline protection is negligible compared to the massive security risk. We recommend optimizing Defender's settings or using a lightweight, trusted third-party alternative rather than disabling protection entirely.

## What should I avoid when looking for tools?
Stay away from "Driver Boosters," "Registry Cleaners," and unverified scripts from Discord or YouTube. These tools often use aggressive, outdated methods that can cause system corruption, blue screens (BSOD), or long-term performance degradation.

## How can I contribute to the guide?
We welcome community contributions that meet our safety and accuracy standards. Please refer to our [Contribution Guide](#contribution-guide) for technical requirements and submission protocols.
